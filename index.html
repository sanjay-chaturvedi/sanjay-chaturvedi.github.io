About Me

I am a PhD researcher specializing in advanced control systems, working towards enhancing the reliability and safety of autonomous systems. My current research focuses on model-based fault detection and diagnosis methods for nonlinear systems, particularly applied to Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs). My work includes developing active and passive fault-tolerant control strategies to improve UAV performance under fault conditions.
During my master's, I designed and developed a functional prototype of a power monitoring system using wireless sensor networks and open-source technologies. This hands-on experience strengthened my skills in system design and practical implementation.
Beyond research, I actively volunteer in areas I am passionate about and enjoy reading non-technical books to broaden my perspectives.
